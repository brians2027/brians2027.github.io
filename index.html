Phases of Matter

Learn how matter changes between solid, liquid, gas, and plasma based on energy and particle motion.

Welcome

The phases of matter are the distinct forms in which matter can be in these states are solid, liquid, gas, and plasma.

Solids

Solids have a definite shape and volume. Their particles are tightly packed and vibrate in place.

Liquids

Liquids have a definite volume but take the shape of their container. Particles move more freely than in solids.

Gases

Gases have no fixed shape or volume. Their particles move freely and spread out.

Plasma

Plasma is a high-energy state of matter found in stars and lightning. It contains charged particles.

Phase Changes

Phase changes occur when matter moves from one state to another due to temperature or pressure changes.
